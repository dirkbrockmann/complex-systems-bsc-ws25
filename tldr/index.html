TL;DR - Course Information

Here’s a summary of the key parameters of the Module Introduction to Complex Systems (M0500-31W20) that is offered as part of the Bachelor Degree program Molecular Biology and Biotechnology at TU Dresden as an elective compulsory module.

Course Details

The course has three components:

  1. Lecture (Mondays & Tuesday 18:30-20:00)
  2. Seminar (Wednesday & Thursdays 18:30-20:00)
  3. Lab Course (Block course, 2 weeks in Jan. 5th - Jan. 15th, 2026).

Lecture

Lecture, Seminar and the Lab Course will be held at Georg-Schumann-Str. 7a at the Center Synergy of Systems (SynoSys).

  • Name: K0500-31W20xV - Introduction to Complex Systems (V)
  • Starting date: Monday, Nov. 24th, 2025
  • End date: Tuesday, Jan. 13th, 2026
  • Number of sessions: 12
  • Date & Time: Mondays & Tuesdays, 18:30 - 20:00
  • Location: SynoSys Seminarraum, Georg Schumanstr. 7a 2. Stock
  • Language: English
  • Max. number of participants: 15
  • Instructor: Prof. Dirk Brockmann

Seminar

  • Name: K0500-31W20xS - Introduction to Complex Systems (S)
  • Starting date: Wednesday, Nov. 26th, 2025
  • End date: Thursday, Jan. 15th, 2026
  • Number of sessions: 12
  • Date & Time: Wednesdays & Thursdays, 18:30 - 20:00
  • Location: SynoSys Seminarraum, Georg Schumanstr. 7a 2. Stock
  • Language: English
  • Max. number of participants: 15
  • Instructor: Prof. Dirk Brockmann, Dr. Pascal Klamser, Adrian Pelcaru

Lab Course

The lab course is a two week block course

  • Name: K0500-31W20xP Introduction to Complex Systems (P)
  • Starting date: Monday, Jan. 5th, 2026
  • End date: Thursday, Jan. 15th, 2026
  • Number of sessions: 8
  • Date & Time: Mon., Tue., Wed., Thu., 10:00 - 17:00
  • Location: SynoSys Seminarraum, Georg Schumanstr. 7a 2. Stock
  • Language: English
  • Max. number of participants: 15
  • Instructor: Prof. Dirk Brockmann, Dr. Pascal Klamser, Adrian Zachariae

Grading

The final grade for the course will be based on three performance parameters

  1. A written exam at the end of the course (50%)
  2. A presentation of a Complexity Explorable (30%)
  3. Participation in the lab course (20%)
